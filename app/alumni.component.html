Alex Cooper studied with Dr. Nuguid (or Horacio) for seven years from age ten to seventeen (2005-2011). Under Horacio's instruction, he was able to develop both technical and musical proficiency that have greatly helped him in pursuing his own career as a pianist. In addition to taking lessons he also gained lots of experience by attending music festivals in Iowa, Northern Minnesota, and Italy. Since auditioning with the first movement of Mozart's Sonata in C Major at the age of ten, he has grown and progressed significantly to where he is now (learning the Bach/Busoni Chaconne in D minor) as an undergraduate studying piano performance at the University of Iowa. After graduating, he plans on applying to graduate schools.

Caitlin Johnson (caitlinrjohnson.com) holds a Master of Music from the Royal Academy of Music in London and a Bachelor of Music in violin performance from Brigham Young University, where she was a presidential scholar with national merit distinction. Caitlin has played violin under maestros Robin Ticciati, Yan Pascal Tortelier, Semyon Bychkov, and Christopher Warren-Green at the Royal Academy of Music and at the Royal Festival Hall. In London, she played for Maxim Vengerov, Gonzalo Acosta, Erich Gruenberg, and Garth Knox. She toured with the Street Orchestra of London, performed at the International Mozarteum Summer Academy (Salzburg) and Rehearsal Orchestra Festival (Edinburgh), and played in the London Musical Theatre Orchestra. Caitlin recently gave a lecture recital entitled “The Finale You Can Smell? Tchaikovsky’s Violin Concerto” in Hong Kong at the 2016 International Conference on Performance and Creativity. While at BYU, Caitlin served as associate concertmaster of the Symphony Orchestra and as a first violinist in the Philharmonic Orchestra. As part of the BYU Chamber Orchestra, She toured and performed in the United States, Europe, and Asia, in venues such as Budapest's Liszt Academy and Beijing’s Poly Theater. Caitlin won first place in the 2014 Utah Music Teachers Association Concerto Competition. She presented her undergraduate thesis research in 2014 at an international musicology conference in Portugal. Her paper, “Tchaikovsky’s Violin Concerto: The Composer’s Original, Auer’s Edition, and the Performer’s Dilemma,” also won first place in BYU’s 2015 Inquiry Journal competition and was published. Caitlin currently teaches violin in Rochester and has plans to pursue a doctorate.
